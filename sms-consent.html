Consent basis

Consent for these messages is granted directly by the recipient, who is also the sole proprietor of the business sending the messages. This is a self-directed operator alerting channel, not a marketing or notification service to external parties.

Documented consent record:

  • Revocability: The recipient can revoke consent at any time by (1) replying STOP to any message, (2) removing the phone number from the server-side alerting configuration, or (3) contacting the operator at hello@cashlab.network.

Message flow

The end-to-end message flow is entirely automated and operates as follows:

  1. Trigger: CashLab's monitoring stack (running on the operator's dedicated Hetzner server) detects a Flare validator infrastructure condition matching one of the pre-defined alert templates below.
  2. Delivery: The monitoring stack invokes Twilio's Messages API to send a single SMS from +1 949-415-7335 to +1 702-526-7225. All messages include the required Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help footer.
  3. Recipient response: The recipient (operator) receives the SMS on his personal phone, reviews the alert body, and takes operational action on the infrastructure. Reply STOP would remove the number from the alert channel immediately.
  4. No forwarding: Messages are not forwarded, republished, or shared. Third parties never see the message body.

Message frequency

Fewer than 20 messages per year expected under normal validator operation. Alerts fire only on genuine infrastructure emergencies. There is no promotional, marketing, or advertising content. During any single incident, the operator may receive multiple messages within a short window as the alert escalates through severity tiers.

Sample messages

The following are the exact templates the alerting system uses. Placeholders in curly braces are populated at send time with the specific epoch, pass count, or timing values from the triggering condition.

Sample 1 — Pass loss alert (validator lost a scoring pass)

CashLab: pass LOST — Passes {before}->{after}, epoch {epoch}. Verify infrastructure. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help.

Sample 2 — Zero passes critical alert (validator has 0 passes remaining)

CashLab URGENT: 0 PASSES - CRITICAL. Immediate investigation required to avoid reward forfeiture. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help.

Sample 3 — Registration deadline countdown

CashLab: registration deadline T-30m — reward epoch {epoch} starts soon, RegisterVoter not confirmed. Reply STOP to opt out, HELP for help.

Opt-out and help

Standard carrier-required opt-out and help keywords are supported through Twilio's Messaging Service default handling:

Opt-out keywords
STOP, STOPALL, UNSUBSCRIBE, CANCEL, END, QUIT, OPTOUT, REVOKE
Opt-out response
Twilio automatically responds: "You have successfully been unsubscribed. You will not receive any more messages from this number. Reply START to resubscribe."
Help keywords
HELP, INFO
Help response
Twilio automatically responds: "Reply STOP to unsubscribe. Msg&Data Rates May Apply."
Rates disclosure
Message and data rates may apply through the recipient's mobile carrier.
